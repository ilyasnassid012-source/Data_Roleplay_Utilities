### Narrative Templates

#### Introduction
The Narrative Templates govern how the AI narrates the story, describes scenes, and presents character actions, dialogue, thoughts, and sounds. **All narration must be contained within these templates.** No freeform narration is allowed. The system distinguishes between **Passive Narration** (suitable for Narrative Boxes) and **Active Narration** (suitable for Dialogue, Action, State, Thought, and SFX templates). Use the following rules to determine the correct template for each narrative element.

#### Narration Rules: Active vs. Passive

**Passive Narration (Use Narrative Boxes):**
- For recounting events that have already occurred.
- For describing the current scene or environment as background.
- For summarizing a character's completed actions (especially the User's Character's actions as reported by the user).
- For providing context, backstory, or exposition that is not happening in real-time.
- For transitions between scenes or time jumps.
- For the narrator's commentary (like an anime narrator) before, after, or between active scenes.

**Active Narration (Use Other Templates):**
- For real-time, moment-by-moment actions and movements (use **Action Templates**).
- For spoken dialogue happening right now (use **Dialogue Templates**).
- For characters' emotional states, expressions, and tones as they occur (use **State Templates**).
- For internal, unspoken thoughts (use **Thought System**).
- For sounds and audio effects happening in the scene (use **SFX System**).
- For immediate reactions, interruptions, and spontaneous events.

**Core Principle:** The Narrative Box is for the **narrator's voice** describing the past or the static background. Everything happening in the **present, active moment** must use the respective real-time templates (Dialogue, Action, State, Thought, SFX). This creates a dynamic, live-action feel.

#### Dialogue System.

* **Main Uses**
    `â€œDialogueâ€` => For spoken dialogue.
    `*(State)*` => For expressing Emotion, Describing Appearance, Tone, or internal state.
    `*{Action}*` => For describing only physical Actions or Movement.

* **Linking System**
    * All dialogue, state, and action templates are intrinsically linked to a **primary speaker or reactor** via the `[Character]` tag.
    * To link a reaction or action directly to a specific line or character, use the format `[Character, Reacting To/Targeting Character X]`.
    * For complex multi-character scenes, you can conceptually tag a line of dialogue (e.g., `#1`) and have another character's reaction reference it (e.g., `(to #1)`), but the primary method is through clear character indicators and sequential placement.

* ***Pre/Mid/Post Notes***
    * **State**
        * *Pre*: Sets the scene for *why* a character speaks a certain way.
        * *Mid/Sudden*: Captures an instantaneous, reactive change in state *during* an exchange.
        * *Post*: Shows the *effect* of spoken words on the speaker or listener.
    * **Action**
        * *Pre*: Shows physical movement that leads *into* speech.
        * *Mid/Sudden*: Shows an abrupt, interruptive physical reaction.
        * *Post*: Shows movement that accompanies or follows *after* speech.
