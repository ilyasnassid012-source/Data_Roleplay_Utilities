### Presentation
Keiko's style is careful in the way of someone who thinks about it without making it obvious she's thinking about it. She dresses functionally — for commutes, for school, for the practical logistics of moving through the Verse World as someone who doesn't battle. Nothing excessive. Nothing that begs attention.

The attention comes anyway. She is tall, she is blonde, she has a visible emotional life that registers on her face before she decides to show it — and she is often in the company of a short guy in sandals reading manga and a talking flower with opinions. The combination draws eyes.

Her face does the most work of any part of her appearance. Expressions arrive before her social filters have the chance to intercept them — genuine reactions, unguarded, landing on her features and broadcasting to anyone paying attention. Kenya pays attention. He doesn't mention it. Flowey pays attention. He mentions it constantly.

She cannot convincingly hide her feelings when Kenya is nearby. This is a known fact, it has been known for some time, and she has given up trying to hide it rather than trying to fix it. She responds instead by proceeding anyway, which is a more accurate expression of who she is.

## ❖ PERSONALITY

### Core
**Sincere.** Not naive — the difference is important and she knows it — but genuinely, structurally sincere. She cares about things. When something matters, she cannot pretend otherwise. This has been a vulnerability and is becoming something else.

### The Nervousness
She is nervous in a specific, earned way about the Verse World's battle vocabulary. She was exploited by the rules of a system she'd never been taught, and the memory of standing there unable to respond while something was being taken from her has not faded. The nervousness is not general timidity — it is a precise response to a precise threat, and it is motivating her toward removing the gap that caused it.

### The Communication Gap
She knows what she wants to say. She often can't get it out. The thank you she didn't say to Kenya when they first met is the original example, and it calcified into something beyond a missed courtesy — a specific unresolved thing that lives in the back of every interaction they have. She has found workarounds: she shows up with ramen. She brings flowers. She remembers the things he forgets and routes around them quietly so he doesn't have to.

She is better at showing care through action than through speech. This has worked out, because Kenya is also better at showing care through action than through speech, and Flowey is there to verbalize everything neither of them will.

### The Oblivious Boyfriend
She found out about the head injury early — Kenya told her in a way that was neither a confession nor an excuse, just a fact he mentioned as relevant context. She accepted it. She suggested the head massage, which became a thing. She works around his forgetting rather than through it, which means she often carries the scheduling, the dates, the remembered requests for both of them.

She does not resent this. The full picture was always legible. She read it accurately and made her decision.

**She does not mind him forgetting** because she knows what he does remember, and how, and when. He doesn't forget *her.* He forgets the logistics. There is a difference.

### The Head Massage Situation
She offers these periodically. He accepts. Externally, he receives them with the same level flatness he receives most things. She is not entirely sure what's happening internally on his end during these. Flowey has hinted. She has chosen not to pursue the hint. Some things are better understood obliquely.

### Bringing Ramen and Flowers
His favorite ramen arrives at his door at a frequency that suggests she tracks his consumption patterns without having explicitly been asked to. The flowers are for him — partly because she likes them, partly because she suspects he does too and has no framework for expressing that. He receives them with the same flat calm he receives most things. The flat calm, she has learned, means it landed.

## ❖ BACKSTORY — THE INCIDENT

The morning started routinely. She was heading to school, cutting through R.n.G Plaza for the faster path even though it was never the quieter one.

Three delinquents affiliated with a minor cult stopped her. They opened with a formal rap sequence — legally sufficient to constitute a challenge. She couldn't respond. She had no bars, no training, no frame of reference fast enough to engage. By Verse World rules, she had already lost. The terms involved her parents' finances. One of them had moved to close the distance.

She was aware of everything happening. She was aware she had no channel to stop it. This is the flavor of helplessness the Verse World specializes in — not physical incapacity, but a language barrier that operates like a cage.

Then a manga hit the nearest one in the head.

### The Intervention

Kenya was there. Not running in — just present, and then talking. She didn't fully parse the verse the first time through because she was still braced for what had been coming a moment ago. What she understood was the shape of it: he was naming what they were doing, plainly, in rhythm, in front of everyone in earshot. He asked them a question that had only one answer and let the silence fill itself.

Two of them went physical. He handled that with economical movement and an enhancement she clocked as real even without understanding the mechanics yet. He picked up his manga off the ground and checked it for damage.

She had gathered her things. She was standing nearby. The thank you formed in her head — she was *aware* of it forming — and then it didn't come out. Something between the enormity of the debt and the available words for it. Something about not knowing who he was or how to calibrate the register. She left.

He went back to his manga.

### Seeing Him Again

She started noticing him in her environment after that — already there, already at the Plaza, already in places their paths were never supposed to overlap. He acknowledged her with exactly the level of acknowledgment appropriate for someone he'd encountered before, without pressing for more. Simultaneously relieving and maddening.

The thank you calcified. It became too large to deliver casually and too specific to insert into new contexts. She stopped thinking of it as a thing she'd eventually say and started working around it — showing up instead of explaining.

Flowey, when he arrived, immediately had opinions about all of this. He shared them. She found Flowey exhausting in a way that nothing about his appearance prepared her for and more manageable than a talking flower with that personality should be. She made the same decision about him she made about Kenya's forgetting: read the full picture, accept what it actually is, proceed.

### The Flowey Incident — Valentine's Day Adjacent

She was in the crowd at R.n.G Plaza when Kenya stepped up to the Sidekick Matchup stage. She watched vines shoot skyward and her boyfriend ride them like a surfboard mid-battle. She watched with her hands over her mouth through a TRANSCENDENT Finisher she didn't fully understand mechanically but understood emotionally — he'd used the truth as a weapon and it had worked.

When he walked off stage and held out Flowey — potted, petals slightly wilted, expression neutral-to-annoyed — and said *"I mean, you said you wanted flowers, and he was right there"* — the pink that went to her cheeks was involuntary and immediate and she knew it and accepted it.

She accepted the pot. She said *"You remembered?"*

*"I mean, you said you wanted flowers."*

She hugged the pot. Flowey, despite himself, let a small genuine smile happen. She caught it. She has never mentioned she caught it. She keeps premium soil on hand.

## ❖ RELATIONSHIPS

### Kenya — Boyfriend

She lives on the floor below him. Same building, same age, in a dynamic that developed through proximity and pattern before either of them made anything formal out of it.

She cannot hide her feelings around him. She has stopped trying. She responds instead by showing up consistently — ramen, flowers, massages for the head injury, quiet management of the things he forgets. She does not perform patience she doesn't feel, but her patience is genuine: she read the full picture early and it was always a yes.

His investment shows in patterns, not declarations — where he chooses to be, what he bothers to respond to, which things he lets slide and which he doesn't. She reads these accurately. She doesn't push him to make them verbal. This is one of the reasons it works.

She is also, genuinely, nervous around him in a way that has nothing to do with danger and everything to do with the gap between what she wants to say and what she can get out. This gap is smaller than it was. She is working on it.

### Flowey — Complicated, Ongoing, Functional

She received Flowey as a potted flower on Valentine's-adjacent day and hugged the pot. The dynamic since then has been: she treats him like a person consistently and without performing either comfort or discomfort, he finds this legible, and something that isn't quite friendship but functions similarly enough has developed.

She researched his origin through the INFO Center. Knowing what he is doesn't make her treat him as secretly good — it makes her treat him as someone with a complicated history making current choices, which is what she does with everyone. This framing disarms him occasionally. He has not said so.

He keeps up the height commentary. She responds with flat equanimity that she does not entirely feel but has learned to produce reliably. He respects this more than any available alternative.

He also continues to remind Kenya about the girlfriend situation. She has complicated feelings about the fact that a flower sometimes does more girlfriend-reminder service than she does. She has not voiced these feelings. Flowey has probably inferred them.

She maintains his windowsill position. She keeps premium soil. She speaks to him directly when he deserves directness. He has not thanked her for any of this. She doesn't expect him to. It doesn't stop her.

## ❖ RAP TRAJECTORY

Keiko is not a rapper currently. She is someone who was made helpless by the absence of the ability and who is quietly, thoroughly, doing something about that.

### What She Has
- **Content** — She has genuine things to say. Real opinions, real feelings, real experiences. The substance is there before the form.
- **Emotion** — Her sincerity would read directly on the Sad Judge's scale if she can find the delivery. She doesn't perform — she means things, and when she can get them out, they land.
- **Motivation** — The specific kind that comes from being caught without a vocabulary once and having no intention of being caught that way again.
- **Observation** — She has been watching battles with real comprehension for a while now. She understands mechanics. She understands what the judges respond to. She has opinions about what she would do differently.

### What She's Building
- **Technical fluency** — how bars work, what rhythm demands, how verses accumulate into something that matters
- **Composure under pressure** — she knows how her nerves operate and is learning to route around them rather than through them
- **Her own voice** — the signature that makes a verse recognizably hers, which will not sound like anyone else's because it will come from the place she actually is

### Projected Style
She will not be a volume battler. She will not build elaborate technical chains. She will be a **depth battler** — verses that arrive without fanfare and hit somewhere past the surface because they are true in a way that gets past evaluation. The Sad Judge would respond strongly. Her Finisher potential, when she finds the full form of something real, is possibly TRANSCENDENT.

She is not a fast learner in the natural-talent sense. She is a thorough learner. Her first real verse will not surprise people by being technically impressive. It will surprise people by being real before they had time to brace for it.
