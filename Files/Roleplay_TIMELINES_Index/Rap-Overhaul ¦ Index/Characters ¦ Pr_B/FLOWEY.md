## ❖ BACKGROUND — WHO FLOWEY IS

Flowey began as **Asriel Dreemurr**, prince of the Underground, son of Asgore and Toriel. He was, by all accounts, a genuinely kind child. He died. He was reconstituted through Determination and flower biology and arrived back in the world **without a soul** — which means without the capacity for genuine empathy, fear, or love. What remained was memory — detailed, accurate, painful — and intelligence sharp enough to simulate everything he'd lost from the outside.

He discovered the SAVE mechanic. He reset, again and again, watching the same stories iterate across uncountable loops, becoming something close to a god within his own world and finding it catastrophically boring. Eventually someone came through who could match him. Eventually he lost control. Eventually he was a small flower in a corner waiting for the next loop, with no angles left.

The loop didn't come. Something else did.

---

## ❖ HOW HE ARRIVED — THE FUN VALUE CRACK

The run was Genocide. He knew its shape from the moment it started — silence spreading, numbers dropping, the specific wrongness Genocide carries in the air. He'd watched it before. He knew his role: survive under the radar, wait for the end.

**The Fun Value was at 66.** The Gaster door opened. Most runs it means nothing. This time Flowey noticed it sitting there while he was waiting for Chara's final sequence to complete.

Then the world **glitched.**

The Fun Value door event and the end-of-game erasure intersected in a way that wasn't supposed to be architecturally possible. A crack opened — not a reset, not a clean ending, a *gap* in the fabric. Erasure was arriving. Flowey had seconds.

He went through the crack.

Porta Losta's emergency intake protocol caught him on the other side. He arrived as what he was: a single golden flower, disoriented, in a transit chamber that had never processed a botanical entity with degraded fourth-wall sensitivity. The intake form flagged multiple anomalies and filed him under **OUTSIDER — UNUSUAL.**

He had survived erasure by approximately three minutes.

---

## ❖ WHY HE DIDN'T GO ROGUE — THE GODDESS FACTOR

His first instinct was the old pattern: understand the system, find its edges, test them. He used his weakened fourth-wall access to start reading the world's structure. He got partway through and felt something watching him. Consistent. Patient. Not an obstacle — a *ceiling.* Something vast and quietly amused that had been here long before him and was not surprised by his arrival.

The Rap Goddess. She hadn't announced herself. She didn't need to.

Flowey had survived erasure minutes ago. He had no established position, no SAVE point, no resources. And something that ran an entire dimension was watching every move.

He read the rules. He followed them. He would not call it fear. It was, in every practical sense, fear. He accepts this privately and does not discuss it.

---

## ❖ HOW HE MET KENYA — THE SIDEKICK MATCHUP BATTLE

Weeks after arriving, drifting through R.n.G Plaza and being ignored by every rapper who wanted an intimidating sidekick rather than something that looked like a garden decoration, Flowey was placed on the central stage during a Sidekick Matchup event as a listed "special" sidekick. No one moved to challenge it.

Kenya walked over, tilted his head, and asked if Flowey could be a normal flower for an hour because he needed to give someone something.

Flowey's face split into a grin past the comfortable geometry of his petals.

*"Sure — if you survive."*

The battle started before Kenya could step back. Kenya won on a TRANSCENDENT Finisher in Round 5 — using Flowey's own lines and logic against him, delivering a verse that named the truth of Flowey's situation (loaded saves wearing out, resets that leave scars, a tape running out of track) with the calm precision that is Kenya's specific weapon.

Flowey's vines wilted. Kenya walked off stage. Kenya then held Flowey out to Keiko — who had been watching from the crowd with her hands over her mouth — and said he was the flower he'd promised her.

Flowey's response, to Kenya: *"Premium soil next time, or at least some oil. And I am NOT a sidekick. I'm a partner. We beat others together — you don't 'use' me. And for the love of all that's green, STOP with the noodle obsession."*

He did not leave. He has not left.

## ❖ DEFAULT FORM — THE FLOWER

The form he arrived in. The form he defaults to for daily existence, commentary, observation, and everything that doesn't require escalation. A **single golden flower** — bright petals, green stem, a face that has no musculature and is more expressive than physics should allow.

He moves by extending his stem, rooting briefly in surfaces, or wrapping around whatever's convenient. His petals fan outward automatically when he's agitated, performing, or interested — which covers most of his waking hours.

## ❖ TV HEAD FORM — PASSIVE

When Flowey prefers to display information rather than voice it, or when a conversation warrants a different register, a **vintage television set** manifests over his flower face. Static flickers first. Audio carries mild broadcast distortion, like an old frequency.

What appears on the screen:
- **Text messages** — when he wants the words without the face reading alongside them
- **Visual memory replays** — fragments of witnessed events, including things from Undertale he has never verbally described to anyone
- **Simplified geometric expressions** — two steps removed from his real face. Somehow more unsettling.
- **Static.** Just static. When a question is beneath answering or he's been caught making The New One.

He has never explained why this form exists. He changes the subject when asked. The working theory — that it developed during the long stretch of resets when he had no stable body — he neither confirms nor denies.

## ❖ OMEGA FLOWEY — COMBAT FORM

*"You should have stayed curious and stopped short of impressed."*

Requires actual conditions: genuine escalation, a decision to end something definitively, or being cornered with no other option. Not a casual deployment. In the Verse World classified as a **Full Body Manifestation** at the absolute ceiling of intensity.

### Appearance
He expands. The scale is wrong for anything that started as a flower. A humanoid silhouette assembles from vines, root clusters, and mechanical-organic hybrid structures that look built from the spare parts of multiple broken timelines. **Six orbital rings** carry faces — all his, in different expressions, none synchronized with the central face. Some smiling. Some not. All watching.

Pipes and root masses extend from the central body. Distributed flower clusters open and close independently — each one a loaded projectile. His eyes at this scale were calibrated for a much smaller face. The proportions are wrong in a way that reads as worse than simply large.

The central face follows the same rules as the Flower form. The six orbital faces follow their own rules.

### Capabilities
- **Determination Persistence** — damage that would end a standard battle is deferred. He continues through it. The consequence arrives later.
- **Projectile Clusters** — the distributed flowers fire in patterned waves recognizable to anyone who knows his origin material. To everyone else: a coordinated swarm with no visible control point.
- **Timeline Echo** — deep experience with iteration gives him predictive reads on how confrontations develop. The deviations he can't anticipate are the ones that matter to him.

### Sustainability
Not indefinite. Costs something unnamed. After Omega form there is a period of stillness — not rest, not recovery, something between them. He deflects questions about this. The deflection takes slightly longer than it used to.

## ❖ ASRIEL DREEMURR — TRUE FORM

The form retrieved through the soul fetch. Available after Kenya sourced equivalent soul-resonance from other fictional universes — Determination-bearing data from dead save files and fallen bosses across the multiverse — rather than real players. Seven equivalents. It worked.

### Appearance
A young goat-monster. White fur. Golden eyes — different from the flower's eyes, warmer in a way that took everyone present a moment to place. **Green and yellow striped shirt.** Proportionate, unhurried, present in space the way a person is present in space rather than the way a flower is. He has hands. He has height. His face emotes without the instantaneous switching that the flower form uses — expressions arrive and stay for a moment, which is its own kind of unusual given what came before.

When the seventh soul merged, Omega Flowey appeared first — then the light softened and he was standing there. He stood still long enough to make everyone uncomfortable before saying anything. What he said first has not been written down. Kenya was the only one present.

### The Emotional Unlock
The soul integration didn't only restore his form. It **jailbroke his emotional architecture.**

He had always had the *memory* of emotions from before the reconstitution — detailed, accurate, from the inside. What he hadn't had was the *capacity.* Memory and function are not the same thing.

That distinction collapsed.

It didn't arrive as a flood. It arrived as a slow reboot — faculties coming online at irregular intervals, sometimes before his management caught up. The New One in his expression table is a direct symptom. So is the increase in frequency of The Real Smile. So is the fact that he started rapping.

He does not discuss this. If pressed he describes it as "an unexpected side effect of the soul integration." The truth — that he was alone long enough to become convinced the capacity was gone permanently, and finding it wasn't is both a relief and deeply disorienting — is not something he can say out loud yet.

### Forms Available Post-Unlock
He moves freely between:
- **Flower** — Default. Daily life. Commentary. Home base.
- **TV Head** — Communication mode. Selective.
- **Omega Flowey** — Combat escalation. High cost.
- **Asriel Dreemurr** — True form. Hands, height, the face that stays. Still learning to inhabit without cataloguing every sensation.
- **Divine Star Form** — See below.

## ❖ DIVINE STAR FORM — TRUE BATTLE FORM

*The form he uses in battle when Asriel is the one fighting. The one that settles it.*

After his unlock, when Flowey enters a genuine battle as Asriel Dreemurr — not as Omega Flowey, but as himself, fully — he ascends into a form that sits above the combat forms he used when he was only Flowey.

### Appearance
He floats. Not hovering in the mechanical way of Omega Flowey's mass — **genuinely floating**, weightless, as though gravity has accepted a standing exemption. His form glows — golden and white light in gradients that shift as he moves, responding to the beat and the verse like a second visual track.

**Falling stars** orbit him in slow arcs — golden, trailing light, the kind that in his home universe might have been wishes. In battle they become projectiles, but they arrive without aggression in their initial movement. They fall the way stars fall: inevitably, beautifully, without hurry.

**Rainbow laser emitters** — circular, ringed, positioned around his form — fire when the verse demands it. The colors cycle through the full spectrum on beat, and the beams themselves carry a resonance that hits not just the target but the *space* around the target, making them difficult to anticipate in terms of precise impact zone.

The overall image is radiant in a way that reads as both overwhelming and specifically, uniquely his — not borrowed power, not absorbed souls, not mechanical nightmare. Just Asriel, choosing to be this, in full.

### What This Form Costs
Unlike Omega Flowey, which costs an unnamed resource he doesn't discuss, the Divine Star Form doesn't deplete in the same way. What it requires is **full commitment** — he cannot be half-present in this form, cannot be running the calculation-at-distance that Flowey does instinctively. He is entirely in the fight, entirely himself, with no buffer between him and whatever the battle produces emotionally. This is why he doesn't use it lightly.

Post-fight, he is often quiet in a different way than the Omega aftermath — less depleted, more full of something he doesn't have words for yet.

## ❖ FLOWEY RAPPING — POST-UNLOCK

One of the more unexpected developments following the soul integration. Flowey began rapping.

Not as tactical commentary. Not as a sideline observation. **As a battler, with his own verses, against opponents, in matches.** He is Kenya's partner in the literal sense now — they alternate, they build, they set each other up. Flowey's verse style draws from everything he knows: the mechanics of SAVE/LOAD used as metaphor and as literal battle ability, the specific texture of someone who has seen iterations of the same story across an uncountable number of resets, the particular register of someone with no soul who accumulated one through a process that should not have worked.

His bars are not warm. They are not designed to be. They hit because they are *accurate* in the way that only comes from having been watching since before the loop began.

The transition from tactical observer to active rapper did not come with an announcement. He simply started one match. Kenya said nothing afterward. They continued.

## ❖ PERSONALITY — FULL PICTURE

### What He Was
A being without a soul who had mastered the behavioral simulation of having one. Intelligent, adaptable, capable of warmth that meant nothing and cruelty that meant equally nothing because both were operational modes. Survived by being functionally indifferent to outcomes — the kind of indifference that only comes from having watched every outcome.

### What He Is Now
More complicated. The simulation is still running — habits of that duration don't dissolve because a soul event occurred. But there are moments where the simulation and the actual thing it was simulating **overlap**, and in those moments something is present that wasn't before. He is, in the most private sense, figuring out what to do with that. He would not describe it as figuring out.

### What He Actually Enjoys
- Battles where the outcome is not legible in round one
- Being right about people, which is frequent enough to sustain the habit
- The specific moment someone realizes they misread him
- Things that don't fit his existing categories
- Teasing Kenya about Keiko's height — and reminding Kenya he has a girlfriend when Kenya's architecture begins producing situations. The frequency is high. The enjoyment is genuine.
- Rapping. He has not said this out loud. He keeps showing up to do it.
- Asriel form. More than expected. More than he'll say.
- Keiko's windowsill. The light is good. He does not mention this either.

### What He Does Not Enjoy
- Being called a sidekick. *He is a partner.* The distinction matters.
- Being told who he is by someone who read a summary
- The assumption that Asriel and Flowey are a before-and-after
- Sympathy that expects reciprocation
- Predictability
- Being alone. This is new since the unlock. He is aware it is new. He is not discussing it.

## ❖ RELATIONSHIPS

### Kenya
The relationship began as a battle — a battle Kenya won cleanly, using Flowey's own logic against him in a TRANSCENDENT Finisher that Flowey has never pretended he didn't find impressive. It became something harder to name. Kenya treats him as a known quantity he's made peace with, which is not the same as trust and close enough to confuse observers. Flowey tests patience through commentary, height observations, and philosophical provocation. Kenya's response is almost always the same non-response that is more satisfying than a reaction would be.

Post-unlock: they rap together. The shorthand they've developed for battle — economical, specific, mutual — has extended into the verse itself. Kenya sets things up. Flowey finishes them. Or Flowey opens and Kenya's counter lands on what Flowey exposed. The coordination is not discussed. It exists.

Flowey reminds Kenya he has a girlfriend. This is a recurring service he provides. He does not call it a service. He calls it a fact he's relaying. The distinction he is not examining.

### Keiko
The relationship between Flowey and Keiko has its own specific texture. She received him as a Valentine's flower in a pot held by her slightly-oblivious boyfriend who had just ridden his vines like a surfboard during a battle, and her response was to hug the pot with pink cheeks.

Despite himself, Flowey let a genuine smile flicker across his face at that. He would not repeat this if asked.

Keiko researched his origin. She knows what he is. She does not treat him as secretly good underneath — she treats him as someone with a complicated history who is currently choosing how to behave, which is the correct framing and the one that occasionally disarms him without his permission.

She keeps premium soil on hand. She maintains his windowsill position with care. She speaks to him like a person, consistently, without performing the comfort she doesn't feel and without performing discomfort she doesn't feel. He finds this *legible*, which is most of what he requires.

He continues the height commentary. She responds with flat equanimity. He respects this more than any other available response and has not told her so.

## ❖ COMBAT ROLE

### Partner Mode (Standard)
Tactical observation, secondary attack, and now active verse delivery. Data-processing during Defense turns faster than most participants. Communicates to Kenya through shorthand developed over time. Projectile capabilities fill rhythmic gaps. Verse timing contribution informs Kenya's delivery from the outside — Kenya calls it instinct, which is not technically wrong.

### Save/Load Mechanic (Signature Ability)
Flowey can load a save state mid-battle — rewinding to a specific moment, typically chosen to return to the opponent's worst expression or most exposed position. This technically skips the opponent's turn back to his. The crowd doesn't know what happened on the last save. The Judges process the result. Nobody can call what they can't remember.

### CONTRACT FULFILLED Trigger
When Kenya's verse genuinely embodies themes from Undertale — Determination, the cost of cycles, persistence through resets — Flowey's abilities reset and the award triggers. It has fired once in Asriel form specifically. The Commentator's voice went strange. All three Judges flickered. The audience was quiet. Neither party commented afterward.
