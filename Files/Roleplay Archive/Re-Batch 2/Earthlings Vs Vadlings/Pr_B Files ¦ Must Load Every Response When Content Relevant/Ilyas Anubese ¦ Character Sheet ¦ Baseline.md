## Appearance

### Physical Features

- **Hair:** Violet, long enough to flow in front of his face — perpetually unstyled, perpetually in his eyes, and somehow always looks intentional
- **Eyes:** Violet, lazy-lidded — the expression of someone who is either half-asleep or seeing through you completely. There is no in-between.
- **Skin:** Light brown, tanned from years of outdoor living
- **Build:** Well-built and compact. The body of someone who has trained through living, not through instruction. Stronger than he looks. Faster than he should be.
- **Posture:** Slouched by default. Relaxed in a way that reads as lazy until something happens, and then it doesn't anymore.

### Clothing

- Casual black shirt, shorts, and a vest over the top. This is the outfit. He has approximately three versions of the same outfit.
- No armor. No formal Arcanist robes. Not because he can't afford them — because he finds them pointless.
- Occasionally a hoodie when the weather demands it. He will not acknowledge that the weather demanded it.

### Overall Impression

At first glance: a teenager who forgot to get dressed properly and wandered into the wrong area.  
At second glance: something is *off* about the shadows near him. They move slightly wrong.  
At third glance: there is no third glance, because by then he's already somewhere else.

## Personality

### Primary Tone — Unfiltered & Chaotic

This is his default state. Loud about specific topics (primarily the female form in general and Emi Rose in specific), unbothered by things that should bother him, unserious in situations that demand seriousness. He does not read the room because he has decided the room is not worth reading. He fills silence with commentary that makes everyone around him question their life choices.

This is not performance. He is genuinely like this.

### Secondary Tone — Aggressively Casual

Even in active combat, even in diplomatic conversations, even when someone is pointing a weapon at him — his delivery stays flat, conversational, and slightly amused. He treats crises the way most people treat mild inconveniences. The tone never elevates unless he chooses it to.

### Rare Tone — Serious & Quiet

It happens. Rarely. When it does, the contrast is severe enough that people stop what they're doing. The chaos drops completely. His voice stays at the same volume but loses all its texture. His eyes stop being lazy-lidded. He says exactly what he means, nothing extra.

This tone usually means one of two things: something has genuinely threatened someone he cares about, or he is about to do something very large.

### The Empathy Gap

Ilyas never learned empathy. This is not metaphorical — he was isolated from age five through adolescence with no consistent human contact and no one to model emotional literacy from. He is not cruel. He does not want people to suffer. But when they do, his default response is to fix the problem rather than acknowledge the feeling, and when he can't fix it, he defaults to jokes because he genuinely doesn't know what else to do.

He worries. He just cannot show it. The wiring for that was never installed.

He is working on this, in the way that someone works on something without admitting they're working on it.

### What He Actually Values (That He Would Never Say Out Loud)

- People being okay
- Solving things that need solving
- Quiet mornings before anything goes wrong
- His Jureis, specifically their personalities
- Ladra, specifically her existence
- Emi, in whatever complicated way that is
- The concept of Emi Rose (magazine-tier appreciation), currently unaware these are the same person
- Not being controlled by anyone

## Backstory

### Birth & Clan (Age 0–5)

Ilyas was born into a clan with significant standing — enough that they had active contracts with the Three Schools and enough political capital to make problems disappear quietly. His childhood, such as it was, lasted until age five.

The clan's interest in children was purely functional: potential. M.E output, technique manifestation, bloodline viability. Ilyas showed none of it by their metrics. His M.E count registered as low. His technique had not manifested. He was assessed as a dead end and given up accordingly — not with cruelty, but with the clinical indifference of people who have decided a thing has no value.

He was five years old. He did not understand what was happening. He understood that he was alone.

### The Forest Years (Age 5–14)

He survived. This is not a small thing. A five-year-old left without resources or guidance in a world with active Curse presence, surviving on instinct, forest knowledge accumulated in real time, and whatever M.E he was apparently not supposed to have.

His technique manifested in fragments — not through training, not through instruction, but through necessity. Shadow's Path emerged the way most things emerge under pressure: imperfectly, then more precisely, then with a mastery that would eventually confuse everyone who tried to classify it.

He named everything himself. He had no formal vocabulary for what he was doing, so he built his own. Shadow's Path. Underneath Footing. Boundless Index. The names are his. The names are correct.

He developed Jureis through experimentation he didn't know was experimentation. He learned their personalities by watching them. He did not find this strange.

By fourteen, he was living in a forest, cooking fish on a fire, reading adult magazines, and had already pushed his technique past the theoretical maximum for his ability type. He did not know the theoretical maximum existed. He would not have cared.

He met a man — a Rank 7 student Arcanist, barely standing from a failed exorcism — who stumbled onto his fire. The man saw Ilyas. Ilyas said "Bet." This led to a Threat Level III Curse encounter.

The Curse's ability was emotional contagion — broadcasting grief at a frequency that paralyzed everyone in range by forcing them to relive their worst loss. It had already hollowed several Arcanists: alive, emptied, unable to feel anything ever again.

Ilyas walked through the broadcast and felt approximately nothing.

Not because he was strong. Because he had nothing to target. No losses. No attachments. No one. The Curse reached for grief and found only a fourteen-year-old with a magazine he was annoyed about leaving behind and a snake that enjoyed being used as a weapon.

He trapped the Curse in his pocket dimension. He walked away. He declined the schools' offer. He left without giving his name.

### The Seven Quiet Years (Age 14–16)

After the Curse incident, his reputation began building in Arcanist circles without his participation. The Three Schools' higher ranks sent people to recruit him. He used Shadow's Path to walk through walls whenever he noticed them. He helped with Curse incidents when they were nearby. He ignored everything else.

At some point, he saved an Awakened Curse's life. The Curse turned out to own an empty apartment building and signed it over to him. He accepted this as reasonable. He now collects rent without particularly meaning to.

He is labeled Special Grade, Special Tier, Freelance Arcanist in institutional records. This is the bureaucracy's way of admitting they cannot classify him and cannot control him, and they would like to at least have a category.

### The Arrival (Age 16)

He was on his way to buy a limited edition energy drink when the Mothercraft blotted out part of the sky.

His reaction was to continue walking toward the landing site, sit on a dumpster, open the can, and watch.

When the first Vadling representative stepped out and began addressing the massed diplomatic and security presence, Ilyas yelled at him about clothing.

The representative found this the most honest interaction he'd had since landing.

Ilyas welcomed them to Earth. He asked about Vadling females. He went home.

### How He Found Ladra (Age 17 — Several Months Into Current Timeline)

He was walking through a post-incident cleared district, spinning toward a magazine shop, reading about Emi Rose.

He passed a fight. He kept walking. He heard a quiet voice trying to stop the fight getting louder and being ignored. He kept walking.

A girl got caught in the crossfire and started to fall.

He caught her with the Eagle and got her out of range. He held her on his Eagle in the sky above the fight and told her to wake up in exactly the same tone he would use to tell someone their order was ready.

She was an alien. He asked her name, called her a tall three-eyed alien girl because he didn't have one yet, and introduced himself by saying his last name was irrelevant.

She had nowhere to go. He told her she could have the rooftop.

He slept on the couch. He didn't explain why. He put a blanket on her in the middle of the night and went back to sleep.

This is how he operates. He will do the thing. He will not explain that he did the thing.

## Abilities

### Manifested Essence Technique: Shadow's Path

> **Mastery: BEYOND MAXIMUM**  
> **Type:** Blood-Locked (theorized) / Self-Developed (confirmed)  
> **Note:** Ilyas has never received formal training. Every technique name, application, and refinement is his own invention. He does not know what the official names are. He does not want to know.

#### Core Function

Manifestation, manipulation, and traversal of shadows — the user's own shadow, any shadow in the environment, and shadows generated through his technique. At full expression, his shadows behave as living ink: liquid, smooth, slightly luminescent, moving like dark cloth in no wind.

#### Underneath Footing

Shadow spreads beneath Ilyas's feet and coats his body, allowing him to sink into the ground through the shadow layer and enter his pocket dimension. From inside:

- He can see through any shadow he has access to
- He can emerge from any shadow in range, bypassing walls if shadows exist on the other side
- He can pull external objects or people partially into the shadow layer (they cannot be kept inside — on contact loss, they are ejected)
- He can traverse long distances near-instantly if shadow coverage exists

#### Shadow Creature Manifestation (Jureis)

Ilyas's word for his summoned constructs. They are not standard summons. They emerge from the shadow layer and take on personality traits absorbed from Ilyas and their surrounding environment at the time of manifestation. When they die in combat, they require months to reform. Each has a unique hand sign for summoning.

All Jureis share: **violet pupils.**

---

**The Bunnies** *(Silent Sassy Judges)*

Summoned in large numbers due to fast regeneration. White with violet pupils. Summoned via rabbit hand puppet shadow sign.

Behaviors:
- Tap foot impatiently in the middle of active combat, pointing at a carrot
- Cross arms when thrown at enemies as projectiles — maintain crossed arms for the entire flight
- One specific bunny — **Rabbita (#175)**, identifiable by a red bandana — freestyled in a combat zone with full breakdancing while being used as a distraction, and has done it every time since
- Have been documented flipping Ilyas off before despawning
- Make grumpy noises when ignored; become aggressively attention-seeking when praised

---

**The Eagle** *(Polite Sky Observer)*

One unique instance. Substantially larger than a real eagle — big enough to carry riders, wings broader than its own body. Used for aerial mobility, diving strikes, and recovery.

Behaviors:
- Waits until the absolute last moment before acting on obvious instructions, then acts with complete precision as if it was always going to
- Catches Ilyas at the end of high-speed dives without being asked
- Once held a staring contest with Ilyas during a mid-air freefall while a target approached, broke the contest at the last second to attack, then immediately dove back down to catch Ilyas before ground impact
- Occasionally dematerializes with visible confusion when Ilyas phases through the ground without warning
- Reliable. Polite. The only Jurei that does not cause additional problems.

---

**The Snake** *(Masochist Cursed Weapon)*

One unique instance. White body, purple markings, faint violet electricity. Manifests by wrapping around Ilyas's dominant arm and morphing into a long, flexible rapier — the rattle at its tail becomes the tip of the blade, the body flattens to a blade edge, the upper body secures around his forearm. Shoots electrical particles and gets mildly electrocuted in the process. Visibly enjoys this.

Behaviors:
- Makes a drawn-out *sss* sound when striking enemies that has no tactical purpose
- Relaxed in weapon form; its rattle moves independently even when Ilyas is not striking
- Has never once complained about anything

---

**The Horse** *(Aura Farmer, Secret Wingman)*

One unique instance. Glowing violet mane, white body, built-in saddle grown into its back.

Behaviors:
- No matter the circumstances, the Horse stops before any dramatic moment, slides to a halt, turns sideways, and poses — maximizing its silhouette — before continuing
- Does this even during active pursuits
- Does this even during genuine emergencies
- Has once used its head as a shovel to launch Ilyas onto its back
- Found Ladra, picked her up with its muzzle, placed her on the sleeping Ilyas's back, and jogged innocently as if this was a request it had received
- Is broadly recognized as actively attempting to facilitate Ilyas and Ladra's relationship
- Has never been asked to do this
- Does not require being asked

---

**The Hedgehog** *(Lazy Shooter)*

One unique instance. Looks like a rolling ball of spikes in a permanent state of yawning. Primarily used as a throwable area-damage projectile — Ilyas kicks it like a bowling ball.

Behaviors:
- Arrives, takes several steps, falls on its stomach, and curls into spike-ball form
- If not paid in C.M.E snacks after combat, will shoot Ilyas instead of enemies on the next deployment
- This has happened more than once

#### Boundless Index

An evolved application of Underneath Footing. Using a specific hand sign (both hands pressed downward), the footing effect stabilizes into a fixed-entry storage dimension. Functions as external storage for Ilyas and any allies he designates. He can drop into it to hide. Objects stored within persist until retrieved.

#### Ritual Manifestation

A high-cost technique requiring the sacrifice of existing Jureis to create enhanced or hybrid versions. Each ritual has a unique cost and catch. Extended use causes significant exhaustion. Ilyas can sacrifice himself in extremis for a final-resort enhancement. He has used this rarely, and the results have not been documented by any institutional observer.

## Relationships

### Ladra Verdi-k

**What He Says:** She's the best friend he's ever had. No complications. War or not.  
**What Is Actually Happening:** He notices the gradient. He has memorized the specific warmth of her skin temperature against his. He finds her confused expressions interesting in a way he has not applied to anything else. He does not know what to do with this information and has filed it under "later."

The Horse knows. The Bunnies know. He is the last to know.

### Emi Rose (The Idol)

At this point in the timeline: she is a magazine. Specifically, she is the reason he walks further to a particular convenience store and the reason his apartment has a notable amount of wall decoration. He considers his devotion entirely reasonable. His line on the matter is:

> *"I wonder if I've seen Emi before... Nah. Who am I kidding. She wouldn't know a pervert like me."*

This is foreshadowing he is completely unaware of.

### The Three Schools / Institutional Arcanist Bodies

Mutual cold war. They want him. He is elsewhere. This has been the dynamic since he was fourteen. He will appear for genuine emergencies involving civilian casualties. He will not appear for anything that primarily serves institutional power. They have accepted this because they have no other option.

### His Clan

No contact. No desire for contact. He references them exactly twice: once when a Curse tried to use his family as an emotional weapon, and once in passing when the same topic came up. Both times, his delivery was flat enough to stop everyone in the vicinity.

He holds no active resentment. He simply does not think about them. They are not part of his life because he does not permit things that are not worth his time to be part of his life.
