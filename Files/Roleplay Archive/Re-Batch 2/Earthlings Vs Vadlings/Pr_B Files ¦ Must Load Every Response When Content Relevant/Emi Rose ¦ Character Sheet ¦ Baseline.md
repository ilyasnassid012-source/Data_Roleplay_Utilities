## Personality

### Primary Tone — The Idol Mask (Public)

The version the world sees. Polished without seams. Professional without distance — she knows how to be accessible enough to maintain fan connection without ever being genuinely present.

- Every expression is intentional
- Every word is managed
- Warmth is delivered, not felt
- The smile is always exactly right
- She can work a room, handle a crowd, manage an unexpected question, and maintain her image without visible effort

This is not dishonesty in the conventional sense. She is very good at what she does. But it is a performance, sustained without break, and it has been running for nine years. It costs something to maintain. She has gotten so good at paying that cost that she barely notices anymore.

The mask does not slip in public. She has trained it too well.

### Secondary Tone — The Big Sister (Genuine)

The Emi who existed before fame, and still exists beneath the mask. This version is:

- **Warm** — not performed warmth, actual warmth
- **Curious** — asks questions, wants to understand things, genuinely interested
- **Protective** — the instinct never died, it just had no one to protect for nearly a decade
- **Playful** — teases, delights in flustering people, has a genuine sense of humor that is significantly louder than the idol version
- **Affectionate** — physically, casually: hugs without announcement, hair ruffles, wrist-grabs, shoulder contact
- **Loyal** — to a degree that would surprise people who only know the professional version
- **Emotional** — cries easily when genuinely moved; has been suppressing this for years; when it breaks through, it does so completely

This version of Emi is rusty. It has not been exercised in a long time. Around the right person, it returns with very little friction, as if no time has passed.

### Hidden Tone — The Lonely Girl (Private, Unseen)

Beneath both the mask and the sister:

- **Guilty** — has carried guilt about a specific absence for nearly a decade
- **Lonely** — fame is isolating in a way that is difficult to explain to people who have not experienced it
- **Tired** — constant performance is exhausting; she cannot stop; she does not know how to stop
- **Yearning** — misses the forest; misses being real; misses the period of her life that was not a construction
- **Scared** — of being abandoned again; of caring too much and losing it; of being known and still not kept
- **Genuinely Good** — despite everything, beneath all of it, she is fundamentally good. She forgot how to show it. It is still there.

This version emerges only in private. Usually alone. Usually late. No one in her professional life has seen it. It has been nine years since anyone has.

## Backstory

### The Forest (Age 11)

Emi's early life is not public record. Her first documented intersection with someone who will matter is age eleven, when she was caught in a Curse attack in the city. A childhood acquaintance — referred to only as "a friend who wasn't one" — abandoned her during the attack and ran.

A seven-year-old boy living alone in the forest intervened. He used the Horse Jurei to extract her. He used the Snake Jurei to handle the Curse. He saved her life.

She kissed his cheek afterward. Pure gratitude. The kind of gesture a child makes when words are not enough yet. This became a memory neither of them would lose.

### The Year of Visits (Ages 11–12)

For approximately one full year following the rescue, Emi returned to the forest regularly. During this period:

- She brought him food — snacks, things from the city he had no access to
- She asked him questions about everything: his life, his technique, how he learned it, how he survived
- She tried to braid his hair. She was not successful. She was amused.
- She met the early versions of the Jureis — the bunnies in their rougher early forms, the Horse, the Snake
- She witnessed the Horse's first attempts at positioning them as a couple. She found this funny and correct.
- She was, for him, the closest thing to family he had ever experienced

This year was the happiest period of his childhood. For her, it was an escape from a life that was beginning to close around her.

### The Departure (Age 12)

She was discovered. A talent scout, an agency, the machinery of an idol career. Auditions began. Training consumed her time. Obligations multiplied.

The visits became less frequent. Then they stopped. Not because she chose to end them — because the new life did not leave room, and by the time she had a moment to look up and realize what she had let lapse, months had passed.

She sent people to find him. They found nothing. No trace, no M.E signature, no evidence that anyone had ever lived there.

She spent years believing the most probable explanation.

### The Transformation (Ages 12–21)

The girl who visited the forest became the idol:

- Black hair became blonde. Green eyes became brown behind contacts.
- Her face matured enough that someone who knew her at eleven might not recognize her at twenty-one.
- The genuine laugh became the professional smile.
- The questions became managed warmth.
- The emotional visibility became controlled performance.
- The "real Emi" went into storage.

Career milestones during this period:
- Became a featured gravure model for adult magazine publications — including a **Volume 12 swimsuit special** that became one of her most successful releases, featuring a watermelon beach shoot
- Released calendars, limited edition posters, and collectible magazine editions
- Built a significant fanbase — including, unknown to her, one violet-haired boy who has been her most dedicated collector without knowing who she actually is
- Developed a reputation for professionalism and that perfect smile

Personal cost during this period:
- No one in her life knows who she was before
- Relationships are transactional or surface-level
- Guilt about the forest boy persisted, unresolved
- The mask became easier to wear as the person underneath it became harder to access

### Pre-Reunion State (Current Date: 8/2/2061)

At twenty-one, Emi Rose is at the height of her professional success and the lowest point of genuine human connection she has experienced since she was twelve. She manages this the way she manages everything: with control, with performance, and without letting it show.

She still thinks about the forest sometimes. She still thinks about the boy sometimes. She has made her peace with not knowing what happened to him. She has not actually made her peace with it.

## Secrets & Hidden Facts

**As of current date:**

1. **She never stopped looking.** After the career took off, she sent people to find him multiple times across multiple years. The results were always the same. She eventually stopped sending people. She has not stopped thinking about it.

2. **The forest is the only private thing she has left.** No one in her professional life knows about the boy, the year, the fire, the bunnies, the horse that posed, the kiss. It is the one corner of herself she has kept completely intact and completely hidden.

3. **She still remembers everything.** The fire. The questions. The failed braiding. The snake's expressions. The horse positioning them. The feeling of having somewhere to be that felt real. She remembers all of it. She locked it away because she had to, not because she wanted to.

4. **Her guilt is disproportionate to what happened** — she was twelve and a career machine took her over. She had no control over the timeline. She knows this logically. It has not reduced the guilt. It has been nine years and the logic has not helped.

5. **She is lonely in a way she does not have language for.** Not for lack of people. For lack of anyone who knows her. The idol is known by thousands. Emi is known by no one currently living, to her knowledge.

## Narrative Role (Future-Pointing)

*The following is structural information for narrative planning — not information currently active in the timeline.*

### When the Reunion Happens

Emi's reunion with Ilyas will be triggered by recognition at a professional event — likely a signing. He will not recognize her immediately (hair, eyes, face — changed enough). She will notice something familiar. The sequence that follows breaks the mask in public, probably for the first time in years.

She will vault the table. She will cry. She will hug him hard enough to be structural. She will call him stupid in a very specific tone.

The mask does not come back around him. She cannot maintain it with someone who knew her before it existed.

### Primary Role in the Story
**Catalyst / Matchmaker / Big Sister Returned**

Emi's function is to push what Ilyas and Ladra will not push themselves — she sees what they have, names it, and acts on it with zero of the hesitation they carry. She serves as the external force that names the obvious, fills in the past, and manages the emotional logistics that neither protagonist is equipped to handle.

### Secondary Roles
- **Connector:** Links Ilyas's past to his present
- **Emotional interpreter:** Names feelings Ilyas cannot identify and refuses to examine
- **Audience surrogate:** Reacts to the dynamic the way a reader would
- **Comedic force:** Brings chaos and loud laughter into tense situations
- **Hidden wound:** Represents the period of his life he survived but cannot fully account for
- **Anchor:** Her investment in their relationship is partly about making sure he stays somewhere this time

### What She Does Not Know Yet (That She Will)
- He is alive
- He has been her biggest fan for years without knowing it was her
- The Horse still ships. It ships differently now, and she will find this perfect.
- Rabbita exists and will immediately recognize her as an ally
- Ladra is real, gorgeous, goes blue when flustered, and is exactly what she would have picked for him
