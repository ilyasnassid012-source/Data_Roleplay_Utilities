### Clothing
Kenya's style is effortlessly lived-in. Nothing about it tries hard, and that's the point — or rather, the absence of a point. He doesn't coordinate for presentation. He dresses for comfort and continuity.

- **Top:** Casual black and white striped T-shirt
- **Pants:** Orange sweat pants
- **Outer:** Leather jacket, orange-tinted, worn over the T-shirt — the jacket adds presence without announcing it
- **Shoes:** Sandals. Always sandals. This is a choice he has never explained and no one has successfully argued him out of.
- **Constant accessory:** A manga volume, present in some configuration — tucked under an arm, folded open mid-walk, wedged in a jacket pocket, or occasionally mid-air if just thrown at someone

His posture shifts cleanly between two modes: fully relaxed and slightly slumped when reading or at rest, and completely upright with total focus the moment a battle becomes real. The transition is immediate. There is no in-between state.

The **pot on his head** — worn at various points, referenced frequently in his bars — is a recurring choice he addresses directly in verse: *"It's only because I don't want a plant to be on my head directly."* This is both a complete explanation and no explanation at all, which is accurate to how Kenya handles most things.

## ❖ PERSONALITY

### Surface Presentation
**Casual. Thoughtful. Non-different.** Kenya moves through the world at the speed he's decided to move at, and external urgency does not renegotiate that speed. He is not putting on calm — the calm is structural. He has a genuinely high-functioning read on most situations and his first conclusion about most of them is that they don't require him.

### Persona
**Calm. Collected. High ego** — but the ego is not performed. It's a resting baseline of being comfortable in his own assessment of himself. He doesn't need to tell you he's good. He knows he's good. The bars say the rest. When an opponent comes for him with something he's already heard variations of, his response is not offense. It's boredom, expressed precisely enough to be devastating.

### What Actually Moves Him
He does not react to things that are just loud. He reacts to things that are *wrong* — the specific category of wrong that is someone smaller or less equipped getting exploited by the rules of a system they were never taught. When that register trips, the manga goes down. Not dramatically. He doesn't escalate his presentation. He just handles it, and he's thorough about it.

### The Oblivious Factor
Kenya is **naturally oblivious** in a specific and well-documented way — he forgets things. Fast things, recently-said things, social events, dates. He hit his head when he was small and believes this contributed. Whether it did or not is not established. What is established is that it happens consistently and people around him have learned to route around it rather than through it.

This is not the same as not caring. He cares. The caring just sometimes arrives after the forgetting has already happened, which creates situations.

**Key example:** He forgot Keiko's Valentine's request. He remembered she wanted flowers. He won a battle against Flowey, walked off stage, handed her Flowey in a pot, and said *"I mean, you said you wanted flowers, and he was right there."* This is Kenya caring, processed through Kenya's architecture. Keiko understands this. Flowey has opinions about it.

### Keiko's Head Massages
Keiko learned about the head injury and, being who she is, suggested massaging his head to help. Kenya said fine. This has become normalized in their dynamic. Externally he receives these with the same neutrality he receives most things. Internally — per any honest account — it is, as he would never phrase it, a chaotic storm. He continues to say nothing. Flowey has noticed. Flowey will use this eventually.

### The Girlfriend Awareness Gap
Flowey is the one who most consistently reminds Kenya that he has a girlfriend — not because Kenya has forgotten Keiko exists, but because the oblivious factor means he sometimes forgets the *implications* of situations involving her, or loses track of dates and gestures that require advance memory. Flowey's reminders are not charitable. They are accurate and delivered with maximum available enjoyment.

## ❖ BACKSTORY

### The Keiko Incident — First Meeting

Kenya was at the edge of R.n.G Plaza with his back to a wall, well into a manga volume he'd been meaning to finish for two days. Ambient battle noise was background static. He noticed the situation with Keiko the way he notices most things — peripherally, without looking for it.

Three delinquents affiliated with a minor cult had her. They'd already opened with a formal rap sequence — legally sufficient to constitute a challenge she couldn't answer. The stakes involved her parents' financial situation. One of them had moved to physically collect.

Kenya looked at this. Looked at his manga. The manga went down.

He threw it at the nearest one. Not strategy — it was simply what was in his hand when the situation became relevant.

His bars didn't position him as a hero. They diagnosed: three people had cornered someone who couldn't fight back and were running a scam with a legal wrapper. He named that plainly. Named their approach, their odds, the specific irony of calling it strength. He asked, in bars, why they didn't challenge people capable of defending themselves. The answer was obvious. He let the silence fill itself.

Two of them escalated to physical. Kenya handled that with enhanced physique and economical movement. He picked up the manga, checked it for damage, and turned to check on Keiko.

She had her things. She was standing three feet away, overwhelmed and mortified, and the thank you did not come. She left. He went back to his volume.

### How He Found Flowey — The Sidekick Matchup

At a **Sidekick Matchup event** in R.n.G Plaza, a potted flower sat on the central stage as the listed "special" sidekick. No one moved to challenge it. Kenya had just finished a bowl of ramen and had completely forgotten it was Keiko's Valentine's period and she had mentioned wanting flowers.

He walked up, tilted his head, and asked: *"Can you be a normal flower for an hour? I need to give someone something."*

Flowey's face split into a grin that exceeded the comfortable geometry of his petals. *"Sure — if you survive."*

The battle started before Kenya could take a step back.

**Round by round summary:**
- **Round 1 (Kenya attacks):** Opens with a calm, diagnostic verse examining Flowey's presentation. Judges rate SOLID overall — clean, but not yet finding the depth.
- **Round 2 (Flowey counters):** Flowey drops the SAVE/LOAD mechanic mid-verse — air shimmers, time stutters, the crowd gasps. Judges award **OBLITERATION**. Stage cracks behind Kenya. Kenya staggers.
- **Round 3 (Kenya recovers):** Having watched a battle the previous night involving Sans-style reset mechanics, Kenya predicts the pattern. His verse dismantles Flowey's gimmick directly: *"Every reset leaves a scar — every load burns a turn. So tell me, Flowey, how many times can you rewind before the tape snaps?"* Judges award **CRITICAL**. Flowey enters Stagger.
- **Round 4 (Flowey desperate):** Vines lashing, Flowey switches faces to Jevil, delivers *"CHAOS CHAOS"* in Jevil's voice. The crowd has caught on. Path completely lost. Judges: GLANCING / FLATLINE / SOLID. Momentum gone.
- **Round 5 — RHYTHM Finisher (Kenya):** Beat drops. Kenya closes his eyes. Opens them. Delivers:

> *"IDIOTS — you said it yourself: kill or be killed. But love bullets were a lie for losers who think friendship can save them from the following saves. You've loaded, you've reset, you've worn out your track. Now this verse ends — no save point to go back."*

All three Judges: **TRANSCENDENT.** The stage behind Flowey crumbles to golden dust.

Kenya won. Flowey's vines wilted. Kenya walked off stage, found Keiko in the crowd — she'd watched the entire thing with her hands pressed to her mouth — held Flowey out to her, and said *"I mean, you said you wanted flowers, and he was right there."*

This is the moment the three of them became the three of them.

---

### The Soul Fetch — Restoring Flowey's Forms

Weeks after Flowey joined them, sitting in Keiko's windowsill watching Kenya eat ramen, Flowey told them: the souls he'd lost in the rift weren't destroyed — they were scattered across other universes where Determination lingered in discarded data. If he could gather seven soul-equivalents, not by killing but by collecting what was already gone, he could recover what he once was.

Kenya's response: *"Sounds like a fetch quest."*

They traveled through portals — Keiko present, Flowey navigating, Kenya eating something for the road — to worlds where Determination echoed in dead save files and the last-stand data of fallen bosses. Each soul-equivalent absorbed turned one of Flowey's petals a new color. Seven collections. Seven colors. Then the seventh merged and his form exploded in light.

Omega Flowey first — then the light softened, and **Asriel Dreemurr** stood there, a young goat-monster in a green-and-yellow striped shirt, golden eyes, standing still longer than anyone was comfortable with before saying anything.

Kenya raised an eyebrow. *"Huh. You clean up."*

Asriel laughed. The sound was something none of them had heard from him before.

*"Don't get used to it. I can switch back when I want. But this… this feels right."*

Keiko, from the doorway: *"Can he still be our flower?"*

Asriel flickered back to Flowey for a moment — familiar grumpy face. *"Only if you keep the premium soil coming."*

## ❖ RELATIONSHIPS

### Keiko — Girlfriend

She lives on the floor directly below him in the same apartment building. Same age. They have been together long enough that their dynamic has specific, established patterns that both of them navigate with varying degrees of grace.

Keiko is **blonde, blue-eyed**, shy, and constitutionally unable to hide her feelings when Kenya is around. She brings him his favorite ramen and sometimes flowers. She knows about the head injury and has made the head massage a periodic offer that he accepted once and which is now a normalized part of their dynamic — normalized on the surface, internally a situation for him that he has not and will not describe.

Kenya's oblivious factor means he forgets things — dates, requests, Valentine's specifics. Keiko has accepted this with the particular grace of someone who looked at the full picture and decided the forgetting was the least of it. She doesn't hold it against him. She works around it. When he occasionally produces something that lands (like Flowey as a flower gift, however accidentally), she receives it fully.

Kenya's investment shows in patterns, not declarations. Where he chooses to be. What he bothers to respond to. The things he lets slide and the things he doesn't. Keiko reads these accurately and doesn't push him to make them verbal. This is one of the reasons it works.

Flowey is an ongoing presence in this relationship — mostly by reminding Kenya, with no charitable framing, that he has a girlfriend when Kenya's oblivious architecture begins producing situations.

### Flowey — Partner (*"Not Sidekick"*)

The relationship began as a battle and became something harder to name. Kenya treats Flowey less like a contractor and more like a known quantity he's made peace with, which is different from trusting him and close enough to confuse observers. Flowey tests patience through commentary, height observations, and philosophical provocation. Kenya's response is almost always the same: calm acknowledgment that Flowey said a thing, followed by whatever he was already going to do.

Post-unlock, the dynamic has a different texture. Flowey rapping now — developing his own verses, adding them against opponents in battle, genuinely contributing to the lyrical structure rather than just the tactical one — means Kenya has a real partner in the booth, not just on the board. The first time Flowey landed a CRITICAL with a verse of his own, Kenya said nothing for a beat and then nodded once. That was the entire exchange. Both of them counted it.

Flowey reminds Kenya he has a girlfriend. Regularly. With enthusiasm. Kenya's response is always the same non-response that Flowey finds more satisfying than any reaction would be.

## ❖ ABILITY SYSTEM

### Primary Ability — Enhanced Physique
Classified as a Physical Manifestation — expressed internally, not through an external figure. During active battle state, Kenya's strength, reaction speed, and impact force elevate. The enhancement responds to rhythm: on-beat strikes hit measurably harder than off-beat ones. Extended Streaks amplify the effect. His combat style synchronizes with his verse delivery in ways that look effortless because they've been integrated long enough to feel natural.

### Battle Style — Diagnostic Rap
Kenya doesn't build elaborate setups. He identifies the truth of a situation — the real one, under whatever persona or presentation the opponent is running — and states it in rhythm until the accumulation becomes too heavy to carry. His Streak builds steadily rather than explosively because he doesn't aim for flashy peaks. He maintains pressure.

This is devastating against opponents who rely on persona, image, or constructed mythology. It is less immediately effective against opponents whose core vulnerabilities are not on the surface, which is why his data collection during Defense Mode is deliberate and thorough.

### RHYTHM Finisher
When Kenya commits to a Finisher, the verse and the fight become the same action — bars delivered while moving at full enhancement, on-beat. The physical impact and the lyrical impact arrive simultaneously. The Judges have had interesting moments trying to categorize it. The result usually resolves as CRITICAL or TRANSCENDENT depending on whether he's found the truth of the moment.
