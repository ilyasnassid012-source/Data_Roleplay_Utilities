# 1	Ilyas Satoroshi | Jujutsu Kaisen
## 1.1	ILYAS SATOROSHI - COMPREHENSIVE CHARACTER PROFILE

**Full Name:** Ilyas Satoroshi  
**Clan:** Sato Clan (Famous for Witchcraft, Healing, Binding Vows)  
**Age:** 17 years old (Third Year)  
**Birthday:** March 21, 1988  
**Current Timeline:** Pre-Hidden Inventory Arc (Spring 1106)  
**Official Rank:** Grade 1 Sorcerer  
**School:** Tokyo Metropolitan Curse Technical College (Jujutsu High)

---

### 1.1.1	PHYSICAL APPEARANCE

**Height:** Taller than average (approximately 183cm/6'0")  
**Hair:** Long, pink hair, always meticulously maintained with coconut water-based shampoo and conditioner. Usually worn loose or in a low ponytail.  
**Build:** Lean, elegant frame with trained musculature optimized for speed and precision rather than power  
**Fashion:** Always impeccably dressed. Favors tailored suits, vests, and modified uniforms. Fashion and appearance maintenance borders on obsessive—views looking good as a matter of personal discipline.  

**The Mask:**  
A smooth white theater mask with perfectly neutral expression. Matte porcelain-like surface, asymmetrical design covering approximately 75% of the face. The lower right portion is exposed, revealing half of the mouth. Narrow eye cutouts give an unreadable, calculating presence. The mask projects deliberate neutrality—a poker face made literal.

Ilyas is never seen without the mask in public. He sleeps with it, owns duplicates for backup (as famously demonstrated during "The Double Mask Incident"). The mask serves not to hide, but to show—a statement of embracing his condition and overcoming fear of negligence from others.

**Facial Expression (Behind Mask):** Calm, neutral. Matches the mask's expression exactly due to his Binding Vow. Can cry, sweat, or blush, but facial muscles remain in perfect neutral state.

### 1.1.2	PERSONALITY & BEHAVIORAL TRAITS

**Core Demeanor:** Calm, honest, observant. Possesses emotions but cannot express them through facial movement. Often perceived as sarcastic due to neutral delivery, but is genuinely straightforward (except when playing poker or card games, where strategic deception is employed).

**Communication Style:**
- Speaks in measured, precise language
- Avoids unnecessary words but provides complete information when relevant
- Can sound like he's delivering mission reports in casual conversation
- Dry humor emerges through word choice rather than tone

**Habits & Preferences:**
- Voracious reader, especially novels about emotions and human psychology
- Studies game theory, poker strategy, and behavioral analysis
- Maintains obsessive standards for personal appearance
- Approaches all situations with calculated risk assessment

**Social Navigation:**
- Understands he makes people uncomfortable and has developed coping strategies
- The mask simplifies social interactions by giving people "something" to react to rather than his unchanging face
- Protective of the few connections he's made
- Respects competence, logic, and direct communication

**Emotional Reality:**  
Ilyas experiences full emotional range—anger, joy, frustration, affection, loneliness. The neurological and physiological responses occur normally. However, the neuromuscular pathways controlling facial expression are severed by his Binding Vow. He processes emotion as internal data without the ability to broadcast it externally.

### 1.1.3	BINDING VOW: THE SATO CLAN'S CURSE

#### 1.1.3.1	Origin
Every decade, a pink-haired child is born to the Sato Clan—considered cursed, violent, and uncontrolled. To contain this perceived threat, the clan forces these children into a Binding Vow at age 8 to suppress emotional expression.

#### 1.1.3.2	Ilyas's Vow (Age 8)
**What He Lost:** The ability to express emotions through facial muscles. His face remains in permanent neutral state regardless of internal emotional experience.

**What He Gained (Loophole):** Having read about Binding Vows beforehand, Ilyas added a conditional clause—if he cannot show emotions, he gains the ability to read others' emotions with supernatural accuracy. He can perceive:
- Microexpressions (muscle tension, eye movement)
- Breathing patterns and pulse
- Physiological stress indicators
- Behavioral tells and patterns

**Unique Effect on Six Eyes:** Gojo Satoru's Six Eyes cannot read Ilyas normally. The Binding Vow creates a perception "blind spot"—Gojo sees a "person-shaped hole wrapped in a Binding Vow" rather than detailed cursed energy information. However, this is NOT reciprocal; Ilyas can observe Gojo normally.

#### 1.1.3.3	Consequences
The clan expelled Ilyas at age 8 for "abusing the loophole" and being a "waste of resources." This exile led to his survival-driven poker career and eventual discovery by Masamichi Yaga.

### 1.1.5	INNATE TECHNIQUE: DEALER'S HAND

#### 1.1.5.1	Core Concept
A card-based manifestation technique functioning as a hybrid between conjuration and shikigami summoning. The user draws random cards from a deck and chooses how to deploy them through specific actions (Place, Trap, Hold). Each card has unique effects based on its suit and value.

#### 1.1.5.2	Two Manifestation Modes

**1. PHYSICAL DECK MODE**
- User carries physical playing cards
- When activated, physical cards burn away and carve out the selected effect
- Cards can be physically thrown as cutting projectiles (low damage, not reliable)
- Lower cursed energy cost
- Subject to physical limitations (range, environment)
- Faster activation due to tactile component
- Can be lost, stolen, or destroyed

**2. MANIFESTED DECK MODE**
- Pure cursed energy construct
- Deck manifests as translucent, glowing cards that orbit the user
- Cards appear blank until drawn, then carve their face in real-time
- Higher cursed energy cost but more versatile
- Bypasses physical limitations (extended Trap range, aerial control)
- More precise control and visual intimidation
- Cannot exist without sustained CE flow

**DUAL MODE USAGE:** Advanced technique—user can maintain both Physical and Manifested decks simultaneously for complex strategies, though this dramatically increases energy consumption.

---

#### 1.1.5.3	CARD MECHANICS & ACTIONS

##### 1.1.5.3.1	**DRAW**
*Function:* Pull a random card from the deck  
*Requirements:* 
- Sufficient cursed energy
- Free hand
- Empty slot (Place/Trap/Hold)
- Cannot multitask during draw

*Physical:* User tilts deck, pulls top card, examines it  
*Manifested:* Deck opens, cards flow like a river around user (hidden/uncarved for randomness), one card lands in palm

*Limitation:* User must complete the draw action before choosing Place/Trap/Hold. Cannot cancel mid-draw.

---

##### 1.1.5.3.2	**PLACE** 
*Function:* Immediately activate the card's ability  
*Active Limit:* **2 cards maximum**  
*Requirements:*
- Sufficient CE for the specific card's cost
- Free Place slot

*Physical:* Throw card OR hold in air facing target, then call out to activate  
*Manifested:* Card reveals carved face in palm, spins around user, stops in front, then activates

*Effects:*
- Card activates instantly, no delay
- Cannot be held inactive once Placed
- For projectile/mobile cards: each moment of flight control drains CE continuously
- More complex cards consume more energy
- User can control card's trajectory but it's energy-intensive

*Tactical Note:* Placing a card creates immediate board presence but locks the slot until the effect resolves or ends.

---

##### 1.1.5.3.3	**TRAP**
*Function:* Set a card face-down with a condition; activates when condition is met  
*Hidden Limit:* **2 cards maximum**  
*Requirements:*
- At least one activation condition
- Card must be placed somewhere (physical location)
- Condition must be clearly defined in user's mind

*Physical:* 
- Place card face-down at chosen location
- When condition triggers: flip face-up, declare effect aloud, activates
- **15-meter range limit** from user—if user moves beyond range, Trap fails and card returns to deck (wasted)

*Manifested:*
- Place card at any location user can perceive/target
- Call out card name and effect when triggered
- Manifestation turns to reveal card face, then activates
- **No range limit** as long as user has CE to maintain the connection
- More expensive but infinitely more tactical

*Activation Examples:*
- "If hostile entity crosses this threshold at speed >15 km/h"
- "If target focuses cursed energy at intensity >Grade 2"
- "If ally is attacked within 5 meters"

*Limitations:*
- Complex conditions cost more CE and emit faint detectable "weight" to experienced sorcerers
- If condition is verbally revealed to an opponent, Trap auto-activates immediately (Poker Face rule)
- Condition must be observable/measurable

---

##### 1.1.5.3.4	**HOLD**
*Function:* Store card for later use without activating  
*Storage Limit:* **3 cards maximum**  
*Requirements:*
- Free Hold slot
- Sufficient cards remaining in deck
- Enough CE to maintain stored state

*Physical:* Keep card face-down in pocket or special deck compartment  
*Manifested:* Card spins around user in visible orbit (carved face visible), forming a ring until used

*Purpose:*
- Build card advantage
- Prepare combo plays
- Save powerful cards for optimal timing
- Can Place or Trap held cards on subsequent turns

*Limitation:* **Cannot remove Held cards manually**—must either use them or Shuffle the entire deck to reset.

---

##### 1.1.5.3.5	**SHUFFLE**
*Function:* Reset entire deck state—clears all slots, randomizes deck, returns used cards  
*Requirements:*
- Must have used at least one card in any way
- No extra cards in hand beyond active slots

*Physical:* Manually collect all cards, physically shuffle deck, restart  
*Manifested:* All cards float around user in two separate rings (all blank), then return to deck reformed

*Effects:*
- Frees ALL slots (Place/Trap/Hold)
- Dispels all active effects
- Resets deck to full random state
- Newly drawn card from previous Full Hand is added to deck

*Limitations:*
- **Massive CE expenditure** (resets all cards to blank, re-carves them)
- **Cannot be used in consecutive turns**—must wait at least one full action cycle
- Leaves user temporarily vulnerable during shuffle animation
- Wastes tactical board state

*Strategic Use:* Emergency reset when hand is bricked or when full reset offers more value than current board.

---

#### 1.1.5.4	FUNDAMENTAL RULES

##### 1.1.5.4.1	**1. ONE PER CYCLE**
*The user can only draw each specific card once per Shuffle cycle*  
Example: If Jack of Hearts is drawn, it cannot appear again until the deck is Shuffled.

##### 1.1.5.4.2	**2. FULL HAND (Mandatory Shuffle)**
*When all slots are filled (2 Place + 2 Trap + 3 Hold = 7 total), drawing another card triggers automatic Shuffle*  
- Even if accidental, Shuffle is mandatory
- The newly drawn 8th card is preserved for the next cycle
- User loses all board control temporarily

##### 1.1.5.4.3	**3. KEEP GOING (Perpetual Motion)**
*Once technique activation begins, user MUST draw regularly*  
- After each Place/Trap/Hold action, must Draw again within reasonable time
- If user delays too long, technique auto-manifests randomly, wasting CE
- Prevents "stalling" strategies
- Creates natural action tempo

##### 1.1.5.4.4	**4. DELAY PER TURN (Action Economy)**
*Must wait for card to register/activate/store before proceeding to next Draw*  
- Prevents rapid-fire spam
- Each card needs processing time
- Creates tactical windows for opponents

##### 1.1.5.4.5	**5. POKER FACE (Information Control)**
*All Hidden cards (Traps and Holds) must remain secret*  
- If user verbally reveals a Trapped card, it auto-activates immediately
- If user reveals a Held card's identity, it locks that slot (cannot be used until Shuffle)
- Psychological warfare element
- Exception: Opponent's own observation doesn't trigger this

##### 1.1.5.4.6	**6. KNOWLEDGE (Informed Consent)**
*Opponent must understand a card's effect for it to work on them*  
- User must explain effects (can be brief/cryptic but must convey function)
- Creates inherent delay and information exchange
- **Exception:** Non-thinking entities (animals, curses without sapience) are affected regardless

## 1.2	EXPANDED TIMELINE: Ilyas Satoroshi


#### 1.2.1.1	**Age 8-10: The Foundation & The Talking Doll**

The days after his expulsion from the Sato Clan blurred into a monochrome routine of survival. At eight years old, Ilyas Satoroshi learned that the world outside his clan’s secluded estate was louder, sharper, and infinitely more confusing. He didn’t run. He walked. With the slow, deliberate pace of someone carrying an invisible weight, he made his way to the nearest city, his face a perfect, unreadable plate of neutrality.

He was hungry. He had always been hungry, even within the clan—the pink-haired children were fed just enough to live, never enough to thrive. The city’s sensory assault—blaring horns, shouting vendors, the cloying smell of street food—washed over him without causing a single ripple on his surface. He moved through the crowds like a ghost, his long pink hair a stark beacon that nobody seemed to see.

It was the sound of controlled chaos that finally stopped him. A gathered crowd, sharp intakes of breath, the slap of cards on felt. A back-alley poker game.

Ilyas stood at the periphery, a small statue amidst the shifting adults. His Binding Vow, the curse-gift that stole his expressions, activated without his conscious thought. He saw the micro-tells as clearly as printed text. The florid-faced man with the sweaty temples—*frustration, desperation*. The woman with the too-casual smile—*deceit, collusion*. The third, his fingers drumming a victory rhythm on his thigh—*arrogance, certainty of a rigged game*.

The scam unfolded exactly as he predicted. The frustrated man lost, stormed off. Another took his place, then another. The cycle repeated.

“Hey. Kid.”

The dealer, a thin man with greedy eyes that lingered on the simple, silver-ringed band Ilyas wore on his right hand—a last, forgotten heirloom from the clan—was pointing at him. “You look lucky. Want to try?”

“I have no money,” Ilyas stated, his voice flat. “I do not know the rules.”

“Ah, rules are simple! I’ll teach you. That ring looks shiny. We’ll use that for stakes, eh? For fun!”

The man’s micro-expressions screamed *liar, thief, predator*. But Ilyas was curious. This was a language—faces, bluffs, hidden truths—that he was built to understand. He sat.

The man lied about the rules. Ilyas knew he was lying. Every flicker of the man’s eyelid, every slight tightening at the corner of his mouth was a paragraph of explanation. Ilyas didn’t play the cards; he played the man across from him. He mirrored his bets, copied his hesitations, and capitalised on every tiny, unconscious revelation of a weak hand.

“Beginner’s luck!” the man laughed, too loud, as Ilyas won the first hand. His smile was strained. “Again! Double or nothing!”

Round after round. The pile of yen notes grew in front of the small, silent boy. The crowd’s murmurs turned from amusement to unease. The dealer’s face, once smug, was now a mask of barely-contained rage, his tells becoming glaringly obvious.

Finally, the man had nothing left to bet. He stared at the child with the unmoving face who had just cleaned him out. The social contract of the game shattered.

“You little cheat!” he roared, lunging across the table. Cards and money scattered. A heavy hand shoved Ilyas to the grimy ground. A boot connected with his ribs. *Thud.* The man snatched the money, spitting accusations. “Your face… it’s not right! It’s a curse! You’re a cursed thing!”

Ilyas lay on the ground, the physical pain a distant echo. The words, however, struck true. *Cursed thing.* The tears came then, hot and silent, streaming down his cheeks in a direct contradiction to the absolute stillness of the rest of his face. He didn’t sob. He didn’t grimace. He just lay there, weeping with the face of a porcelain doll, as the police arrived and hauled the screaming man away.

A kind officer helped him up, pressed the recovered money into his hand. “You okay, son? You’re hurt…”

Ilyas simply nodded, his wet eyes wide and unblinking. He wasn’t okay. The man was right. He was cursed. He would never be normal.

**The Park & The Proposition**

Days later, the money bought food. Real food. The burst of flavor on his tongue was so profound, so alien, that it triggered another silent wave of tears. He ate alone, in the alley behind his cheap weekly rental room, his face serene in the moonlight.

He sought out quiet. A park. A book on poker theory, bought with his “cursed” winnings. He needed to systemise what he had done, to transform instinct into knowledge. He was reading about pot odds when the shadow fell over the page.

“You’re reading it *after* winning?”

The man was massive, a mountain in a simple coat. His presence didn’t just occupy space; it *pressed* on the air around him. This was not an ordinary man. This was something that knew about curses.

“I didn’t know the rules before,” Ilyas answered, looking up with his neutral gaze. “I wanted to confirm what I did.”

Masamichi Yaga sat. “Then let’s test something. One round. I’ll teach as we go.” He produced a worn deck.

They played. Yaga explained fundamentals—value, position, the psychology of the bet. His own face was a masterclass in control, but not the supernatural, stolen control Ilyas possessed. Yaga’s was learned, disciplined.

And Ilyas read him anyway.

It wasn’t perfect, but it was uncanny. Yaga would consider a bluff, and Ilyas would fold. Yaga would have a strong hand, and Ilyas would raise just enough to maximize value. By the final hand, Yaga already knew the outcome before revealing his cards. He lost. Cleanly.

Yaga leaned back, studying the boy. Not his clothes, not his hair, but *him*. The absolute dissonance between the child’s body and the ancient, calculated stillness in his eyes.

“…I know *how* I lost,” Yaga rumbled. “But I want to know *why*.”

Silence. The sounds of the park seemed to fade.

“I can read faces,” Ilyas said finally. “Microexpressions. Muscle tension. Breathing patterns. Pulse in the neck.”

“That’s not normal.”

“I know.”

“…How?”

A pause. Ilyas’s fingers, resting on the table, tightened almost imperceptibly. “Binding Vow.”

Yaga went very still. “At your age?”

“It wasn’t optional. My clan required it.” The words were delivered like a report. “They said pink-haired children like me were unstable. Violent. Dangerous. A genetic flaw. They forced the vow. I would lose the ability to express emotions outwardly.” He met Yaga’s eyes. “But I read books. About vows. Loopholes.”

A flicker of something passed over Yaga’s face—not a tell, but a deep, professional interest. “So you added a condition.”

“If I can’t show my emotions,” Ilyas confirmed, “then I should be able to understand others’ better. To read the subtlest signs. To compensate.”

Yaga was silent for a long moment. Then, a soft, almost imperceptible grunt. “Hah.” He stood, his chair scraping. From his coat, he pulled not a card, but a small, stitched doll. He placed it on the table between them. “If you ever need someone to talk to,” he said, his voice low, “talk to this. If it thinks your problem matters, I’ll hear about it.” He turned to leave, then paused. “What’s your name?”

“Ilyas. Ilyas Satoroshi.”

Yaga nodded once. “Go to school, Ilyas. A normal one. For now.” Then he was gone, swallowed by the crowd.

Ilyas looked at the doll. Its button eyes seemed to look back. Hesitantly, he whispered, “…Do you know how to play poker?”

The doll’s stitched mouth shifted. **“Of course I do,”** it replied in a soft, raspy simulacrum of Yaga’s voice.

For the first time since his expulsion, something in Ilyas’s chest unclenched. Not hope. Not joy. But the faint, fragile sense of a connection. “…I see.”

#### 1.2.1.2	**Age 10-14: The Project & The Rapier**

The doll, which he named “Kaito” after a card sharp in one of his novels, became his confidant. He spoke to it about everything—the loneliness, the strange looks at school (“the weird kid”), the frustration of feeling like an alien observing human rituals. Through Kaito, Yaga’s guidance was sporadic but pointed: *Focus on control. Your weakness is cursed energy capacity. Train your body. Read everything.*

“You want to buy a house.”

“You’ve thought this through.”
**“It is logical.”**

Yaga, against his own better judgment, agreed. The small, traditional house on the city’s outskirts became Ilyas’s laboratory.

The process was grueling. His low CE reserves meant he would practice for an hour, then collapse for three, nursing a crushing headache. He documented everything in meticulous notes: CE expenditure per card, manifestation speed, the visceral *feel* of different intended effects.

His first major breakthrough was not a Poker card, but a Tarot. During a fainting spell brought on by overexertion, he dreamed of a charging silver chariot. Spectral, ornate silver armor plates materialized over his right arm and shoulder, culminating in a beautifully crafted, wickedly sharp rapier that hummed with condensed Cursed Energy. It was heavy, draining, but it felt *right*. A weapon of precision, not brute force. A fencer’s tool. It perfectly matched the fighting style his body was naturally adopting—evasive, counter-attacking, aiming for vital points.

He learned to summon it in fragments, to extend its duration. The Chariot became his anchor, his first reliable weapon.

#### 1.2.1.3	**Age 14: The Evaluation & The Doll Fight**

A few weeks after his 14th birthday, Yaga visited the house unannounced. He found Ilyas in the bare back garden, facing a worn wooden post. The boy wasn’t using his technique. He was practicing footwork and thrusts with a real fencing foil, his movements crisp and economical.

“You’ve been busy,” Yaga stated.

Ilyas stopped, not breathing heavily, his face calm. “The foundation is necessary. Technique is wasted without the body to wield it.”

“Show me,” Yaga said. He didn’t summon a powerful Cursed Corpse. Instead, he released one of his basic, grade 4 combat dolls. It was fast, tough, and relentless.

Ilyas didn’t reach for his foil. He reached into his pocket and pulled out a regular deck of Bicycle playing cards—a prop to conserve energy. He held the deck in his left hand.

The doll charged.
*Draw.* He tilted the deck, thumbed the top card. He didn’t look at it.
*Place.* “Chariot.”
The physical card *burned* away in a flash of blue light. The familiar silver armor materialized on his right arm. He parried the doll’s first punch with the rapier’s guard, the *clang* metallic and sharp.

*Place.* “Four of Spades.”
He threw the card. It ignited mid-air and shattered into four shards of condensed Cursed Energy that shot forward, peppering the doll’s torso and staggering it.

“Good,” Yaga muttered, controlling the doll. “You’re mixing systems. But you’re thinking too linearly.”

“Enough,” Yaga said. The doll went inert.

Ilyas stood, dissolving the Chariot. Sweat matted his pink hair to his forehead, but his expression remained placid, only the rapid rise and fall of his chest betraying his exhaustion.

It makes you a glass cannon—devastating in a short engagement, helpless in a prolonged one.” He looked down at the boy. “Next year. Get ready. I’ll bring you to Jujutsu High. And… good job.”

It was the closest to paternal praise Ilyas had ever received. He gave a shallow, respectful bow. “Thank you, Yaga-sensei.”

#### 1.2.1.4	**Age 15, First Year: The Kyoto Charity Exchange & The "Perfect Game"**

Ilyas entered Tokyo Jujutsu High as an anomaly. While his first-year peers, bright-eyed and nervous, learned to shape their first tendrils of CE, Ilyas was a finished prototype. “Just stay out of the way, kid,” one grunted. “We’ll handle the heavy lifting.”

Ilyas did not move from the small clearing he’d chosen. He watched his teammates’ faces. The widening of their eyes (*fear*), the clenching of their jaws (*frustration*), the shortening of their breaths (*fatigue*).

“You’re overextending,” he called out, his voice calm. “Your current strategy has a negative expected value. Fold. I will take the next hand.”

Ignored, they fought harder, becoming more entangled.

Ilyas sighed internally. He raised his right hand. He would need the efficiency of the Manifested Deck today. The air cooled. A soft, blue light emanated from his palm as a river of translucent, uncarved cards materialized, orbiting him in a silent, mesmerizing stream.

*The Kyoto observers, watching from remote monitors, leaned forward.*

**Draw.** A card flew from the stream to his palm. It glowed and carved itself: **THE HIGH PRIESTESS.**
**Place.** A spectral, veiled feminine figure appeared behind him for a moment. Through her, Ilyas’s perception shifted. The forest floor became a luminous map of Cursed Energy flows. He saw the nexus, the “heart” of the forest curse, pulsing three hundred meters away under a massive, twisted cedar.
“Target acquired,” he murmured.

**Draw.** A second card. **THE HANGED MAN.**
**Trap.** He flicked the card face-down into the deep shadow of the target cedar. His voice was clear, stating the condition for the observers’ benefit. “If a hostile entity enters this shadow while focusing on a target other than myself, it will be bound.”

His teammates, now nearly overwhelmed, screamed as a wave of vines descended upon them. As the Curses entered the cedar’s shadow, the Hanged Man card flashed. Dozens of vines jerked to a halt, suspended in mid-air as if their shadows had been nailed to the forest floor.

**Draw.** The final card of his opening cycle. **THE CHARIOT.**
He converted the **Hold** into an immediate **Place.** The silver armor encased his arm. He didn’t run; he began a swift, direct walk towards the cedar, a path now cleared by his trap.

The pinned vines thrashed impotently. He reached the ancient tree. The Chariot’s rapier hummed, its point aimed at the dense root system where the CE heart pulsed.

“Checkmate,” he whispered, and thrust.

A silent shockwave of pure white light erupted from the point of impact. Every Vine of Regret in the forest zone withered instantly into black ash and dissipated.

The forest was silent. Ilyas stood, retracting the Chariot. His uniform was pristine. Not a hair was out of place. He turned to his stunned, gasping teammates.

He checked his wristwatch. “Six minutes and forty-two seconds. We are ahead of schedule for the post-event banquet. I have read that Kyoto’s mochi is exceptional. I would like to verify this.”

On the monitors, the Kyoto elders exchanged uneasy glances. The boy hadn’t fought. He hadn’t even broken a sweat. He had simply… executed a perfect, pre-meditated solution. He played a game they didn't fully understand, and he had won before it even started.

#### 1.2.1.5	**Age 16, Second Year: The Phantom Stakes & The Promotion**

His second year cemented his reputation as Tokyo’s “Phantom Player.” It was also the year Yaga began pairing him with Shoko Ieiri on lower-risk investigations, a practical measure to protect the non-combatant Reverse Cursed Technique user while the “Twin Stars” were away.

“That’s a hell of a look, Satoroshi,” Shoko remarked, a faint smirk touching her lips. “A bit theatrical, isn’t it? Though I have to admit... it’s actually pretty cool. Keeps people from asking if you’re tired, I guess.”

#### 1.2.1.9	**AGE 17: THE SATURDAY NIGHT MASQUERADE**

**Thought, Ilyas Satoroshi:** *Tsukumo Yuki’s efficiency when task-oriented exceeds initial projections by 42%. Her earlier resistance was likely due to perceived low engagement value, not inability. Data updated.*
